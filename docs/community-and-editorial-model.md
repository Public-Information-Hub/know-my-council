# Community and editorial model (direction)

KnowMyCouncil is intended to be community-contributed and trustworthy. This document describes a plausible future model for contribution and editorial review. It is a planning document, not a promise of implemented features.

## Goals

- Make it easy for the public to report missing information and broken sources.
- Encourage contributors to submit evidence, not rumours.
- Support journalists and researchers with traceable records.
- Keep the project non-partisan and focused on verifiable facts.

## Contribution types

- **Code contributions:** improvements to the software.
- **Documentation contributions:** clarity and accuracy of project docs.
- **Data issues:** missing data, broken links, suspected incorrect records.
- **Investigation leads:** patterns or concerns that need evidence gathering and validation.

## Editorial concepts (future)

### Evidence submissions

Submissions should include:

- primary sources (council pages, official registers, released documents)
- dates and context
- what the submitter believes the evidence shows (clearly separated from the evidence itself)

### Review

A lightweight review process may include:

- verifying that sources are primary and accessible
- checking that claims match the sources
- labelling uncertainty where it exists

### Trust and reputation

Trust mechanisms may include:

- visible contributor history (issues/PRs, not personal identity)
- role-based permissions (contributors, reviewers, maintainers)
- flags for "source needed", "disputed", "needs review"

We should be careful not to create systems that encourage harassment or doxxing.

### Moderation

Moderation should aim to:

- keep discussions respectful (see [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md))
- prevent partisan campaigning and personal attacks
- focus attention on evidence quality and reproducible analysis

### Disputed claims

When evidence is disputed:

- record the dispute without amplifying allegations
- request additional sources or council clarification
- keep a clear timeline of what is known and what is uncertain

## Escalation paths

At early stage, escalation is likely to be:

- discuss in an issue/discussion thread
- maintainer judgement on what can be published as fact vs what remains an open question

Over time, the project may formalise:

- editor roles for evidence workflows
- a documented appeals/dispute process
