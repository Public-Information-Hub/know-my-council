# FOI / EIR direction (planning)

Freedom of Information (FOI) and Environmental Information Regulations (EIR) requests are often required to obtain information that is not proactively published by councils or is published in unusable form.

This document outlines how KnowMyCouncil may support FOI/EIR activity in the future. It is planning only, not implemented functionality.

## Goals

- Help people identify missing public information and transparency gaps.
- Help draft clear, specific requests that are more likely to succeed.
- Track requests and responses in a structured way.
- Store released documents and link them to the records they support.

## Potential features (future)

### Identify missing information

- Per-council dataset checklists (what is expected vs what is published)
- Source tracking with timestamps (last seen, last updated)

### Draft requests (assistive)

- Templates for common request types (spend, contracts, assets, pay)
- Guidance on making requests specific and testable

This should avoid presenting legal advice. It can point to official guidance and explain trade-offs.

### Track and publish responses

- A record of request text, date submitted, and response status
- Attachments and released documents stored with provenance
- Redaction handling where required

### Extract structured information

Released documents often contain valuable information in unstructured form.

Future work may include:

- extracting tables and fields
- linking extracted data to canonical entities
- retaining the original document as the authoritative source

## Legal and ethical boundaries

- Do not publish personal data unless clearly lawful and necessary.
- Respect copyright and licensing constraints on documents.
- Prefer linking to primary sources when retention is not lawful or practical.
