# ADR-0013: Contributor Roles and Trust Model

Status: accepted
Date: 2026-04-04

## Context

KnowMyCouncil is intended to be community-contributed. Community contribution is a strength, but also introduces risks:

- low-quality or careless submissions
- popularity-driven dynamics that undermine evidence standards
- inconsistent moderation decisions

For a civic transparency platform, trust must be earned through evidence discipline and reviewability, not volume or status.

## Decision

Adopt a role model and progression principles that support evidence-led contribution.

High-level roles (conceptual, not implemented yet):

- **Member:** anyone participating (issues, discussions, reports). No special privileges.
- **Contributor:** submits PRs, documentation, or data/evidence issues in a structured way.
- **Editor:** trusted contributor who can help validate evidence, improve provenance links, and curate normalised mappings.
- **Moderator:** trusted role focused on conduct, safety, and enforcing evidence vs assertion boundaries.

Progression principles:

- progression is based on demonstrated evidence discipline, careful review behaviour, and respectful collaboration
- trust is earned through reproducible work and correct sourcing, not popularity
- roles and permissions should be transparent and reversible if standards slip

Moderation expectations (high-level):

- uphold non-partisan, evidence-led standards
- be conservative with unverified claims (ADR-0012)
- protect privacy and prevent harassment

## Consequences

- Contributor workflows and tooling should be designed to support review and provenance, not gamification.
- Editors and moderators will need clear guidance and escalation paths (without pretending a large organisation exists today).
- This role model should be revisited as community scale changes; updates should be recorded as new ADRs.
